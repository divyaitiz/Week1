Layer 3 — because it revealed that "recommendations" aren't one AI model, but an entire system of models working together under extreme constraints.

I assumed YouTube recommendations were just "show me similar videos," but the complexity floored me — it's not one model, but a two-stage pipeline that must narrow 100 million videos to 500 candidates in 20 milliseconds, then rank them while juggling six conflicting goals (watch time, satisfaction, diversity, creator fairness, safety, freshness). The real shocker? This multi-objective balancing act at massive scale is still an unsolved problem for everyone except maybe 3-5 companies on Earth. The moat isn't the AI itself — it's the ability to do this filtering dance in the time it takes you to blink.
